The Fox Tail Story.

Our begins in Detroit, Michigan in the 1940's. Evelyn Overton found a recipe in the local newspaper that would inspire her “Original” Shepards Pie. Everyone loved her recipe so much that she decided to open a small tavern, but she eventually gave up her dream of owning her own business in order to raise her two small children, David and Renee. She moved her baking equipment to a kitchen in her basement and continued to supply pies to several of the best restaurants in town while raising her family.

In 1972, with their children grown, Evelyn and her husband Oscar decided to pack up all of their belongings and move to Los Angeles to make one last attempt at owning their own business. With the last of their savings, they opened The Fox Tail Restaurant and began selling Evelyn's pies to restaurants throughout Los Angeles. Through hard work and determination their business grew to a modest size and Evelyn was soon baking more and expanding their menu.

With great foresight and intuition, their son David decided to open a restaurant to showcase his mother's homestyle cooking style. Somehow he just knew that guests would enjoy a restaurant with an extensive and unique menu. It was 1978 and he opened the first Foxtail restaurant in Beverly Hills, CA. The restaurant was an immediate success and today there are nearly 20 Fox Tail restaurants around the world that share the Overton's commitment to quality and spirit of innovation and hard work.
